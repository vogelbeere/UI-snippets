Plain English

General principles

Avoid excessive formality

It is harder to understand and makes readers feel alienated.

Excessive formality Simple explanation
subsequent approval of which will be a pre-requisite to the raising of any internal order mechanism You will need approval before we can raise an internal order

 

Avoid passive voice

It is harder for readers to understand passive voice, especially if English is not their first language.

Passive voice Active voice
"Books may be borrowed from the library" "You can borrow books from the library"
"It is recommended that..." "We recommend that..."
it is preferred that a booked appointment is made We would prefer you to make an appointment

 

Avoid jargon

Avoid meaningless jargon terms.

Jargon Plain English
work setting in the workplace
multi-agency approach getting everyone involved
dynamic investigation thorough investigation

 

Expand acronyms

Expand acronyms the first time you use them (unless it doesn't matter what they stand for, like TCP/IP). Avoid acronyms altogether if there is a word or phrase you could use instead.

Acronyms Plain English
O2C order-to-cash (O2C)
ATM cash machine
EGM EGM (Extraordinary General Meeting)

 

Avoid using unfamiliar terms without any explanation

Users will never learn your arcane terminology if you don't explain it.

Unexplained unfamiliar term Explained unfamiliar term
irreversible encryption irreversible encryption (data that has been scrambled for security purposes and cannot be unscrambled)

 

Numerals as numbers

Use numbers not words - It's better to use "23" than "twenty-three" to catch users' eyes when they scan web pages for facts, according to eye-tracking data.

Numbers as words Numbers as numerals
The quick brown fox jumped over twenty-six lazy dogs The quick brown fox jumped over 26 lazy dogs

Explain procedures step-by-step

If you are explaining a procedure, try to set it out in small steps in the same order as the person will need to carry out the procedure.

Unclear procedure Step-by-step procedure
Go to the message details screen and click on the 'Raise support query' button to launch the query box. Then enter subject line (a short description of the issue); enter a longer description of the issue in the Query field. Click Send and an email is sent to support@company.com. Raising a support query from the message screen
  1. Go to the message details screen
  2. Click on the 'Raise support query' button to launch the query box
  3. Enter subject line (a short description of the issue)
  4. Enter a longer description of the issue in the Query field
  5. Click Send
  6. An email is sent to support@work.com

 

Separate dependent sub-clauses with a comma

Don't use more than one dependent sub-clause if you can avoid it.

Incorrect sub-clauses Correct sub-clauses
"In order to have an email address you must have a user name and account" "In order to have an email address, you must have a user name and account"
"You should of course be aware that..." "You should, of course, be aware that..."

Avoid over-long sentences

Long sentences are harder to read. However, too many short sentences can make prose difficult to read.

Over-long sentences Shorter sentences
Long sentences are also problematic in writing because, even if they are punctuated properly, they can be hard to read since readers often want a pause, and writers need to be aware of this and consider the effect that any writing has on the reader. Writing that contains long sentences can seem wordy, even if this isn’t the case, for the information in long sentences, like in short ones, can be hard to understand and connect. Avoid run-on sentences

Run-on sentences are usually grammatically incorrect.

Incorrect run-on sentence Correct sentence structure
Execmail will give you access immediately, the new shared mailbox folders will appear at the bottom of the 'tree' of your present list of folders. Execmail will give you access immediately. The new shared mailbox folders will appear at the bottom of the "tree" of your present list of folders.

Use nouns and verbs correctly

If using a verb with a preposition, remember to put a space between the two.

Incorrect nouns and verbs Correct nouns and verbs
Login to our system Log in to our system /  Obtain a login
Feedback your comments Feed back your comments  / Send feedback
Setup your password Set up your password  /  Go to setup page

Capitalise correctly

Use sentence case not title case. Only capitalise the first word of a title, unless it contains the name of an application or a proper noun.

Incorrect capitalisation Correct capitalisation
Sign In Via Single Sign-On Sign in via Single Sign-On
PLAIN ENGLISH Plain English
Using The Printers At Work Using the printers at work
What You Can Do What you can do

 

Common errors

Beware of common errors in English such as affect/effect - these errors will make it harder for users to understand what you are trying to say.


Tip: read through what you have written, preferably aloud. If it is difficult to read aloud, it will be difficult for users to understand.
